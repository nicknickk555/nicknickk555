Abstract

This technical overview introduces Venturi Carpet Technology (VCG) as a novel approach to root cultivation, transforming random air‑pruning into a predictable and controllable process. By engineering the microclimate of roots through geometric design, VCG establishes a framework where sidewall profiles, hydrothermal spikes, and bevel angles act as instruments of cultivation rather than sources of variability.

The document outlines the theoretical basis, key formulas, and structural parameters of VCG, followed by comparative analysis against standard containers. Special emphasis is placed on the predictability of drying probability, the optimization of bevel angles (15°–25°), and the energy efficiency of root guidance.

Ultimately, VCG demonstrates how geometry can serve as a living instrument, enabling growers to achieve consistent outcomes and minimize risk. The executive summary provides a client checklist, ensuring practical applicability and clarity for decision‑makers.

Page 13: Quick Field Check & The 50% Rule

UNITED ROOT • Venturi Carpet Technology (VCG)

1. Quick Field Check

  • Touch test: If the cone wall feels cool/damp beyond mid height, reduce fill next cycle.
  • Visual cue: If white, bushy laterals are forming inside the cone volume, you are in the optimal pruning band.

2. How to Fill the Cone — The 50% Rule

Air, Angle, Root — One System. The cone guides roots toward the air hole and triggers natural pruning. How you fill it with compost determines where and how pruning happens.

  • Geometry effect: Flatter bevel increases fill shelf → raises ℓf, porous thickness, and Rp.
  • Venturi acceleration: Narrowing accelerates airflow → increases kg, Daeff ∝ ueff, via Sherwood number:
    Sh ∼ a · Re^m · Sc^(1/3),   ueff ≈ u₀ / cosθ
  • Net effect:
    • Horizontal segments ease filling but increase Rp.
    • Venturi acceleration reduces Ra, Rh.
    • Balance determines pruning location s*:
      • If Rp dominates → pruning shifts outward (later, softer).
      • If Ra, Rh reduced strongly → pruning shifts inward (earlier, sharper).

✨ The new bevel is not just a usability feature — it is a regulator of pruning dynamics.
